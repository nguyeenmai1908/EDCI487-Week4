When Solutions Repeat Old Patterns
~7 minutes

We've talked about greenwashing as a corporate communication problem - misleading language, false labels, absent accountability. But some false solutions go deeper than bad marketing. Some repeat patterns that look a lot like colonialism.

This section introduces a concept that will appear in your assigned reading this week, and that connects directly to the justice frameworks from Week 3.

Key Concept
Carbon Colonialism

The way wealthy, high-emitting nations in the Global North address their climate responsibilities by shifting burdens - and costs - onto communities in the Global South that did least to cause the crisis.

Source: Parsons, L. (2023). Carbon Colonialism. Manchester University Press.

The Logic of Carbon Offsets

Carbon offsetting lets companies pay for environmental projects elsewhere instead of reducing their own emissions. In theory, the emissions are "cancelled out." In practice, the logic breaks down at every step.

How The Promise Works

Where The Logic Breaks
01
Emissions still happen
The original emissions still enter the atmosphere even if a project is funded elsewhere.
02
Offset impacts are overstated
Projects often exaggerate how much carbon they actually prevent or absorb.
03
Burdens get displaced
Land and communities in the Global South can absorb the costs of a problem they did not create.
The Bigger Pattern

Carbon colonialism isn't only about offsets. It describes a wider set of ways wealthy nations shift climate costs onto others.

🏗️
Exporting emissions
Carbon-intensive industry moves to countries with weaker regulations — then only domestic emissions appear in climate targets, hiding the full picture.
💰
Dependency by design
Climate finance arrangements are structured in ways that create new economic dependencies rather than genuine partnerships between equals.
🌏
Solutions without consent
Large-scale renewable projects are imposed on Indigenous lands without meaningful community consultation or consent.
