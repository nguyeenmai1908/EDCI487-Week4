Module Overview

Why justice is inseparable from climate

"The climate crisis is a justice crisis. The communities that contributed least to the problem are suffering most from its effects."

Before you arrive in class this week, this module will ground you in the foundational concepts of climate justice — a framework that connects environmental harm to systems of race, class, gender, and colonial history.

As a future teacher, you'll leave with language and frameworks to bring these conversations into your own classroom — acknowledging complexity while remaining student-centred and trauma-informed.

Key Concepts

Three frameworks for this week

3 of 3 concepts
Concept 01
Intersectionality & Climate
How overlapping systems of oppression — race, class, gender, disability — shape who bears the greatest burden of climate harm. No single identity tells the whole story.
Concept 02
Climate Apartheid
The emerging divide between those wealthy enough to escape climate impacts through technology and migration, and the billions left behind to endure floods, heat, and food insecurity.
Concept 03
Environmental Colonialism
How colonial histories continue to shape environmental harm today — from resource extraction on Indigenous lands to disproportionate pollution in communities of colour.
